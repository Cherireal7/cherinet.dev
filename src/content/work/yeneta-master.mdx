## The opening

Most school information systems sold in Ethiopia are foreign software bolted to a local market — single-campus by assumption, English-only by default, priced for institutions that don't exist here. We built Yeneta Master for the school that actually does exist: two to twelve campuses under one administration, Amharic on the parent-facing surface and English on the internal one, fee structures that have to flex for siblings and scholarships, and grades that mean different things in different programs.

## The architecture decision

**Multi-campus is in the data model from day one.** A *school group* contains *campuses*, each campus has its own staff, classes, calendar, and fee schedule, but they share student records when families move between them. Trying to retrofit this onto a single-tenant SIS later is hopeless — we made it the foundation.

The stack is **Laravel** on the backend (PostgreSQL primary, Redis for cache and queues, Cloudflare R2 for student/staff documents and gradebook exports), **React + Tailwind** on the front-end, **Inertia** as the bridge so the rendering is mostly server-driven without the SPA tax. Reports and bulk operations get queued; long-running PDF generation never blocks a user.

## The interface

I lead the design system across the product. The screens have to absorb a lot of density — a grades view, an attendance grid, a fee ledger — without becoming a wall of cells. The decisions that buy back the headroom:

- **One accent color used sparingly.** Severity comes from typography and position, not from a status palette.
- **Tabular numerals everywhere** financial or grade data appears, so columns align without manual width-tuning.
- **Sticky context** — when an admin drills into a student, the breadcrumb stays anchored so they never have to recover where they are after three taps.
- **Mobile-first parent views, desktop-first admin views.** Parents look at this on phones in the evening. Admins look at it on laptops during the day. The same backend, two different layout grammars.

## What's in scope

- **Records** — student, guardian, staff, payroll
- **Attendance** — per-class, per-period, with bulk corrections
- **Grades** — configurable per program (national curriculum, IB, private)
- **Fees** — sibling discounts, scholarships, payment plans, reconciliation
- **Communication** — Amharic SMS + email + in-app notifications
- **Reports** — Ministry-of-Education-ready exports

## What's live and what's next

The platform is in development for the first-campus rollout. Marketing site at **[yenetamaster.net](https://yenetamaster.net)**. The next milestone is a live pilot with a Bishoftu school group whose admin team has been part of the design conversations from week one — the SIS that lands isn't going to be a surprise to them.
