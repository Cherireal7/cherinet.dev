## The problem

Starting a registered business in Ethiopia means navigating a fractured paper trail: the Ministry of Trade, the city revenue office, the Ethiopian Investment Commission, sector-specific regulators. The information exists. It is just scattered across thirteen government PDFs, two Telegram groups, and the memory of one accountant who knows three of you.

BizBridge collapses that into a single product surface. Type your sector. Get the checklist. Book the expert. Pay. Track status.

## What I work on

I lead the frontend across a **Turborepo monorepo** — `apps/web` (Next.js 15 + Payload v3 CMS embedded), `apps/api` (Fastify + Drizzle ORM), `packages/shared` (Zod schemas), `packages/ui`, `packages/config`. The frontend ties them together — search, sector pages, expert booking, payments, and the dashboard customers use after they pay.

The hardest part is not the framework choice. It is the **bilingual content model**. A sector's requirements need to render in Amharic or English without translation drift, and the checklist for a downtown Addis salon is genuinely different from one in Bishoftu — so the data model has to carry location-specific overrides without forcing editors to author the same content twice. The Ministry of Revenue source documents live in `docs/mor/` and feed a typed seeder into the database.

## Decisions worth naming

- **Payload v3 over headless WordPress.** Type-safe content, native Postgres via Drizzle, and a UI editors actually use without training.
- **Better Auth over Clerk / Auth.js.** Self-hosted, free, no per-MAU pricing in a market where unit economics matter early.
- **Neon serverless Postgres.** Scale-to-zero pricing for a product still finding its base load.
- **Server-rendered sector pages.** SEO is the channel; pre-rendering localized sector pages with proper metadata is non-negotiable.
- **MeiliSearch for sector lookup.** Typo tolerance and Amharic tokenization out of the box, hosted lightweight enough to run alongside the API.
- **Chapa + Stripe in parallel.** Chapa handles ETB locally; Stripe handles diaspora and partner payments in USD.
- **Cal.com for expert booking.** Self-hostable, embeddable, doesn't lock the booking flow inside a third-party domain.
- **BullMQ on ioredis** for any work that shouldn't block a request — payment reconciliation, document parsing, email batches.

## What's live

Sector search, sector landing pages with cost ranges and checklist, expert directory, and the booking flow are wired through to payment. The post-purchase dashboard is the next milestone — tracking license status without forcing customers to log back into government portals.

## Why this work matters

A platform like this does not need to be flashy. It needs to be **right** — to load on a four-year-old Android in a coffee shop with 3G, to be readable in Amharic by someone who left school at fifteen, and to never lose a paid booking. Designing for that constraint set sharpens every choice you make on a typical SaaS product.
