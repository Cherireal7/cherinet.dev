## Why this page is short

Most of the work I do at Doxa is for clients who would prefer their roadmap not appear on a public portfolio. I respect that, which is why this page summarizes rather than showcases.

If you are a hiring manager and want a specific walkthrough — architecture, decisions, code samples — reach out by email. I can talk through what I built, how I built it, and what I would do differently, in a private call.

## The shape of the work

- **Greenfield product surfaces** — building from a Figma file to a deployed Next.js or React Native app, owning everything from token system to release engineering.
- **System recoveries** — taking over codebases that have outgrown their original authors and rebuilding the parts that matter without a full rewrite.
- **Design system work** — establishing a component library, typography scale, and motion system that a client team can maintain after handoff.
- **3D and motion-heavy interfaces** — when the brief calls for product surfaces that feel premium, I bring Spline, Lottie, and Framer Motion to the table.

## What stays consistent across engagements

- **TypeScript everywhere**, with strict mode on.
- **Tokens-first**, not hex-everywhere. Theming changes touch one file.
- **Accessibility passes** before "done", not after.
- **A single source of truth** for content — usually a typed config or a headless CMS, never duplicated copy across files.
