## What we ship

Doxa's offerings are deliberately narrow: **website development, software solutions, AI automations, IT support, branding and identity, digital content, social media, paid ads.** We don't pitch capabilities we can't deliver senior on.

A typical client lands one of three packages:

- **Starter** — basic site, domain, hosting, from $1,700.
- **Growth** — custom web app, CMS, branding, three months maintenance — $4,500.
- **Custom** — fully tailored web/mobile/cloud, on request.

Monthly retainers from $500. Maintenance from $100. Mission-driven SMBs, nonprofits, and US-based partners. Annual revenue brackets from startup to $5M.

## The stack we standardize on

Across eight shipped projects, the recurring pieces are **Tailwind CSS** on the front, **React** or **Next.js** in most application work, **Laravel** as the dominant backend, and **PostgreSQL** or **MySQL** for persistence with **Redis** for caching. Storage and media go through **Cloudflare R2** and **Cloudflare Stream**. We reach for **Livewire** when the page is mostly a CMS surface (Scholten Pattern Works), **Next.js + GraphQL** when streaming video and content density matter (MySeed), and **React PWA** when the deployment context is offline-first (ZOA Ethiopia).

We choose Laravel over a Node back end deliberately. The talent pool we hire from in Bishoftu and Addis Ababa is deepest there. Replacement cost is low. The framework is opinionated enough that the second engineer to touch a feature inherits the conventions of the first.

Each of these has its own design system, content model, and pace. The unglamorous discipline at Doxa is that we don't reinvent the foundation per project — every new build inherits patterns from the last three.
