## Why this site matters

LHF places confessional Lutheran books — catechisms, hymnals, devotionals, theological primers — into the hands of pastors and congregations in nearly 100 countries. The Ethiopia arm carries that mission into the highlands and lowlands here at home. The website's job is small and specific: tell donors what their gift does, let pastors and bookstores request titles, and surface the publication catalog in the languages people actually read.

I'm not a neutral builder on this one. I'm a theology student at **Maor Lutheran Seminary** in Bishoftu — itself an LCE institution — so the work sits inside a tradition I belong to.

## The design call

Mission sites have a particular failure mode: they read either as church-bulletin amateur or as corporate-NGO sterile. I aimed for a third register — restrained, reverent, scholarly. The kind of weight a 19th-century catechism has on a shelf.

Palette stays tight and deliberate:

- **Heritage blue** `#1B3A6B` for chrome and headings
- **Gold** `#E6A817` — Ethiopian-flag-derived, used sparingly for the donate CTA
- **Green** `#0F8A4C` for confirmation states only
- **Cream** `#FAF6EE` page background; **charcoal** `#0E1525` body text

A tri-color stripe (the Ethiopian flag) appears only in the footer as a decorative accent. Beyond that, no color noise.

## What's on the site

Seven routes carry the work: **home**, **about** (the parent foundation, the Ethiopia arm, the LCMS lineage), **publications** (the catalog by language), **projects** (active translation and printing efforts), **news**, **donate**, **contact**. Content lives in a typed TypeScript module right now and is being migrated to a small CMS layer behind Drizzle so editors can update titles, languages, and donation context without touching code.

The donate flow is intentionally not a checkout. LHF's giving still routes through the US foundation — `Lutheran Heritage Foundation, 51474 Romeo Plank, Macomb, MI 48042` with a memo of "Ethiopia." The site's job there is to make the case carefully before handing off, not to imitate a Shopify store.

## What was hard

**Multilingual rendering across scripts.** Amharic and Tigrinya are Ge'ez script; Afaan Oromoo, Somali, Sidaama, and Wolaytta use Latin with diacritics; English is the donor-facing default. Each needs the right font, the right line-height, and copy that wasn't translated by machine. We're staged through three of the six languages with native review for the rest.

The other quiet problem was tone. A donor in the US Midwest and a pastor in Wolayta are reading the same site. The page architecture lets each find what they're looking for without making the other feel out of place — the donor reads English without seeing untranslated placeholders; the pastor reads Afaan Oromoo without the page feeling like an afterthought translation.

## What's live and what's next

Marketing surfaces, the publications catalog skeleton, and the bilingual structure (English + Afaan Oromoo) are live. Amharic and the four remaining languages roll out as native review wraps. The donation flow ships behind a feature flag, coordinated with LHF US so the campaign attribution stays clean.
